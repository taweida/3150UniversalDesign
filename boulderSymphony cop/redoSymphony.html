Light

April 8, 2022; 7:30 PM

Grace Commons Church- 1820 15th St, Boulder, CO 80302

Pianist, Bryan Wallick joins Boulder Symphony to perform Brahms’ 2nd Piano Concerto

Program:

  • The Moldau by Bedrich Smetana


  • Hymn to the Nordic Lights by Tõnu Kõrvits


  • Piano Concerto No 2 by Johannes Brahms


Musical light from across the spectrum, from Brahms' Piano Concerto no. 2, with Tõnu Kõrvits' Hymn to the Nordic Lights. The vastness of space and expansion of time created by both composers cuts across the threads of genre and style. Tõnu Kõrvits reflects the mysterious patterns and gestures of the Nordic lights, redefines the palette of a symphonic composer.

Brahms creates new worlds with his music, honing the technique and sculpting from by-gone eras to create the future, his concerti are pinnacles of achievement in the symphonic world - Bryan Wallick, Gold medalist of the Horowitz Piano Competition and one of the great American virtuosos of his generation will make his Boulder Symphony debut.

Bryan Wallick

pianist

Bryan Wallick is gaining recognition as one of the great American virtuoso pianists of his generation. Gold medalist of the 1997 Vladimir Horowitz International Piano Competition in Kiev, he has performed throughout the United States, Europe, and Africa.  

Mr. Wallick made his New York recital debut in 1998 at Carnegie’s Weill Recital Hall and made his Wigmore Hall recital debut in London in 2003.  He has also performed at London’s Queen Elizabeth Hall with the London Sinfonietta and at the St. Martin-in-the-Fields Church with the London Soloist’s Chamber Orchestra.
